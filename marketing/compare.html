Why this matters now

AI coding agents (Cursor, Claude Code, Copilot) generate more PRs than any team can review carefully. The failure mode isn't "the code is broken" — it usually runs. It's scope drift: the AI built something adjacent to what you specified, dropped an edge case, or quietly widened access. Diff-only review doesn't see that. Spec-aware review does.

FAQ

Is this a Cursor/Copilot replacement?
No. Those are IDE assistants that help you write. Conclave runs at the PR layer — one review per push, as a merge gate. They're complementary: write with your agent, gate with Conclave.
Do I have to write a PRD?
No — Conclave reviews diffs out of the box. Attach a PRD (or a short spec) and you additionally get scope-drift / spec-mismatch flags, which is where it pulls away from diff-only tools.
